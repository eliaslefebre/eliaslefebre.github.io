Overview

This project was created with the goal of investigating how Extended Reality (XR) could be integrated into the process of mixing spatial audio in a Digital Audio Workstation (DAW) and exploring the potential benefits of such a setup.

To conduct this research, I developed an XR application in Unity that connected to a DAW (Reaper) over a local network using OSC (Open Sound Control).

Below, you can find a video showcasing the final result. On the left, you can see the spatialization plugins that exist in the DAW. These spatialization plugins are visualized in the XR interface as orbs around a head that represents the listener. Each orb spatializes an instrument, with the instrument's name displayed below the spatialization window.

On the right, you can see a simulation of what one might experience in the XR interface. The track being moved in this demonstration is the drum track. With headphones, you can hear the spatialization effect as the orb is moved.

Context:

This project was created as part of my graduation work.

I thoroughly enjoyed my graduation work because of the autonomy it provided and the opportunity to push boundaries with creativity.

Tools used:

  • C#
  • Unity
  • OSC (Open Sound Control)
  • PlugData
  • Reaper
  • Dolby Atmos Plugins

Reflection

I am very proud of this work as it allowed me to step out of my comfort zone. Planning-wise, I was a bit overconfident with what I could achieve in the given time. While I had to cut away a few features, the core idea ended up working fine.
