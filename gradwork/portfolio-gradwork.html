Graduation Work:
Enhancing Mixing of Spatial Audio with an Extended Reality Interface

Home Portfolio Contact

Role: Graduate Researcher

Overview

This project investigates the integration of Extended Reality (XR) technology into the spatial audio workflow within Digital Audio Workstations (DAWs). The primary goal was to enhance visualization, interaction, and immersion in the mixing process.

The video below shows the XR-interface in action. The track being moved manually is the track for the drums.
If you have headphones you will be able to hear the drum be moved in reference to the head in the middle.

Goals and Objectives

  • Explore how XR can improve the process of visualizing and managing spatialized audio.
  • Explore and document how an XR interface can be used with pre-existing DAWs.
  • Assess the benefits and challenges of an XR interface compared to traditional interfaces.

My Role

  • Designed and implemented an XR interface compatible with DAWs.
  • Conducted literature review and technical analysis of spatial audio technologies and XR.
  • Developed and tested a case study application integrating Unity, OpenXR, and audio plugins like Dolby Atmos Composer.

Methods and Tools

  • Unity
  • OSC
  • Dolby Atmos Composer plugin
  • Meta Quest 3
  • PlugData (Pure Data VST)

Key Results

  • H1: XR significantly improved visualization of spatial audio elements, enabling simultaneous viewing and manipulation of multiple objects.
  • H2: While XR provided an immersive experience, it lacked the precision and intuitiveness of conventional methods.
  • H3: The XR setup incurred higher costs and technical complexity, with challenges in configuration and maintenance.

Impact

  • Developed an open-source XR tool for DAWs, providing a framework for future researchers and developers.
  • Demonstrated the potential for XR to revolutionize spatial audio workflows, inspiring innovation in immersive audio technologies.

Reflection and Learning

  • Learned the importance of project scope management and user feedback in research.
  • Highlighted the need for future studies focusing on usability and automation in XR applications for audio production.
